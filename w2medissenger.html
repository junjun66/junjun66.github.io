Medissenger

Medissenger is a project carried out in SJTU Interaction Design Institute. It is a new service system addressing the problem of medication non-adherence, which gives a nudge in altering people’s medication activities to healthier lifestyle.

Duration
  • Jun - Sept, 2016
Team
  • Wangshu Cheng, Junhang Yu
  • Jinchun Wang, Chenhao Jin
  • Wenjun Zhou
Tools
  • Illusrastor, Photoshop,
  • Premier, Animate

Background

Taking medications is a complex and dynamic behavioral process. The word “adherence” refers to taking the right dose of the right drug at the right time.

Medication non-adherence is a public concern which leads to rising health care costs, unnecessary changes to the treatment plan and patient outcomes.

Available Methods

Traditional pill bottles and medicine dose boxes overrate patients’ perseverance. The complexity of written information set barriers to their medication adherence.

Electronic reminder systems can remind patients of their medication schedule, but cannot provide a convenient way to manage so many kinds of pills.

New pharmacy solutions (e.g. PillPack, Webster-pak) build clear information architectures to simplify medication managements, but have weak effect on motivating patients to take pills.

Problem Statement

Inconvenience:

Reading the information on the pill package is complex. It's annoying to manage varieties of medication

Forget to use or refill:

Taking medicine is easily forgotten. People miss the correct time for taking pills from time to time.

Lack of motivation:

In some cases, people do not forget. They simply feel unpleasant or useless about taking medication.

Design Goal

According to core factors in problem statement, three design goals were set for poor medication adherence. We aimed to build a medication management system to motivate patients to start, and to stick with their prescriptions.

1. Simplify medication management and information

2. Provide a reminder for taking medicine

3. Increase the motivation of medication-taking behavior

Brainstorm

Concept

A new medication service system supporting safer and easier processes on both the pharmacy and the patients side.

The form of carrying information was further developed. We defined the industrial design of the pillbox, the sensing system and digital interface of pillbox.

Pillbox and Package

RFID reader identifies the medication schedule; Sensors track the medication behavior

Digital Interface

An e-ink screen on the pillbox conveys information and motivates patients’ behavior change by real-time feedback. I introduced a virtual pet as the role of a reminder and designed a set of animations. The reminder sends intended messages to patients in an encouraging, caring and natural way.

Final Model

Scenario 1 - Initiation

Once you receive a pill pack, you may also get a new pet, for instance, a fish. The fish will stay in the box for a couple of weeks until the current package is empty.

Scenario 2 - Reminder

The fish reminds you any right time for you to take the pills. If you take the pill in time, the fish will be very happy.

Scenario 3 - Deep Reminder

If you forget to come, the fish will be mad and anxious.

Scenario 4 - Reward

You may get a reward after completing the medication.
